Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common inquiries

How can my organization partner with ReliefConnect?

We welcome partnerships with NGOs, government agencies, and corporate organizations. Please contact our Partnerships team at partnerships@reliefconnect.org with details about your organization and proposed collaboration. Our team will review your inquiry and schedule an introductory call.

What types of donations do you accept?

We accept monetary donations through our secure online platform, which provides the most flexibility for our response efforts. We also accept in-kind donations of specific relief supplies during active disaster responses. Please check our Current Needs page for updated lists of requested items.

How can I volunteer with ReliefConnect?

We have both field and remote volunteer opportunities. Field volunteers require specific qualifications and experience in disaster response. Remote volunteers can assist with translation, data analysis, and other support roles. Visit our Volunteer page to view current opportunities and submit an application.

How is my donation used?

85% of donations go directly to program services, including disaster response, preparedness programs, and community resilience initiatives. 10% supports organizational operations, and 5% goes to fundraising efforts. We publish annual financial reports with detailed breakdowns on our website.

How do I report a disaster in my area?

For immediate life-threatening emergencies, please contact your local emergency services first. To report a disaster to our network, call our Emergency Hotline or use the Crisis Reporting tool on our website. Provide as much detail as possible about location, type of disaster, and immediate needs.
